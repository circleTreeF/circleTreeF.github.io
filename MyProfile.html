Study of Conformal Predictor       Jun, 2020- Oct, 2020
  • This research project is supervised by Dr. Tony at the University of Nottingham Ningbo China, School of Computer Science.
  • One journal paper is expected for this research project.
  • Conformal predictors are useful algorithms for making predictions with confidence by predicting sets of values rather than making point predictions. They have the property that they are reliable to some user-defined confidence level, so long as data is independent and identically distributed (iid). These algorithms would split the samples data into 3 data sets, the proper training set, the calibration set and the testing set.
  • However, in the real world cases, the size of the available data set may be too limited to split into three significant data sets. This leads to the research challenges in two directions: (1) to ensure that their predictions are optimally efficient, (2) since inductive conformal predictor require three independent data sets, proper training, calibration and testing, how best to manage the allocation of data to those three sets whilst maintaining the reliability property.
  • This research project addresses those problems with the target to provide a modified version of conformal predictor by overlapping the proper training set and the calibration set and recommendations on their use.
